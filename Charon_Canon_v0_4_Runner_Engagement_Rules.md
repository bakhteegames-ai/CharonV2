# Charon Canon v0.4 — Runner Engagement Rules

## Purpose
These rules define the minimum engagement requirements for the runner loop.  
They affect pattern design, readability, and pacing — not the frozen movement canon.

---

## 1. Decision Frequency
The player must make regular decisions.

Target:
- first 30s: 12–20 meaningful actions per minute
- 30–120s: 18–30 meaningful actions per minute
- later: scaled through difficulty/content pacing

Meaningful action includes:
- lane switch
- crouch / slide
- jump (v3 only)
- risk-route commitment

---

## 2. Anti-Autopilot
The player must not be able to survive by sitting in the center lane for long.
Generator/content should avoid:
- long center-safe corridors
- static repeated symmetry
- low-pressure dead zones > 3–4 seconds

---

## 3. Readability
Obstacles must be readable in about 200–300 ms.

Requirements:
- silhouette contrast
- color contrast
- early overhead telegraph
- no blend-into-track problem

This is especially important in Phase 2C visual polish.

---

## 4. Coin Purpose
Coins are not decoration.

They must do at least one of:
- safe route telegraphing
- tempting risk route

Target mix:
- 70–85% safe guidance
- 15–30% risk route temptation

---

## 5. Rhythm
Desired rhythm:
flow → tension → relief → flow

Breathers are allowed, but should not become boredom.

---

## 6. Variant-specific pressure
### v2
- no jump
- pressure comes from lane decisions + overhead timing

### v3
- jump available
- jump-only content allowed
- can tolerate slightly more vertical decision variety

---

## 7. Visual packaging constraint
Visual style must never kill gameplay readability.
Comic/manhwa identity should enhance atmosphere, menus, transitions, and framing — not obscure obstacle reading.

---

## 8. Practical review questions
Before approving any visual pass, ask:
- Can the obstacle be read instantly?
- Do coins still guide movement?
- Does v2 feel clearly different from v3?
- Is the player readable against the track/background?
- Did style reduce clarity?
